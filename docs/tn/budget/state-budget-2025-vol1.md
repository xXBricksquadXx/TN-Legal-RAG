## Practitioner summary
Volume 1 presents the Governor’s **recommended FY 2024–2025 state budget** and the detailed program statements by functional area. It totals **$52.6B** across all funds, reflecting tempered revenue growth, and makes notable investments in **K-12 (TISA & teacher pay), state employee salaries, TennCare, workforce, safety, parks/outdoors, and mental health**—all **without drawing on reserves**. The **Rainy Day Fund** is planned to reach **$2.07B** by June 30, 2025. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

## Key points
- **Total recommended budget:** **$52.6B** — **$25.4B** state appropriations, **$19.8B** federal, **$5.2B** other departmental, **$2.1B** tuition/fees, **$87.7M** bonds. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}  
- **Policy emphasis:** Education (TISA increases, teacher pay), TennCare, workforce, safety, mental health, and significant **parks/outdoors** capital. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}  
- **Reserves:** Revenue Fluctuation (“**Rainy Day**”) Reserve projected **$2.07B** at 6/30/2025; TennCare reserve **~$1.5B** maintained. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}  
- **No reserve drawdown**; administration frames budget as conservative amid slower growth. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}  
- Document includes **Budget Overview, Revenues, Financials, Capital**, and **Program Statements** (General Gov’t, Education, Health & Social Services, Law & Safety, Resources & Regulation, Transportation/Business/Econ Dev). :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
