## Tennessee differences (high yield)
- Courts apply strict scrutiny of propensity risk; trial court must find a material issue other than conduct in conformity and conduct a robust 403 balance on the record.
- Limiting instructions are routine; notice to defense is expected where feasible.

### Q&A (anchor)
**Q:** What showing is required to admit 404(b) other-acts in Tennessee?
**A:** A material non-propensity purpose plus an on-the-record 403 balancing; court typically gives a limiting instruction.

**Q:** Give two proper 404(b) purposes.
**A:** Motive and identity (among others like intent, absence of mistake).
